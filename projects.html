Australia Redcross Life Blood
Designing a new digital experience for Lifeblood Australia to encourage current donors to continue donating.

1 min summary

Problem: Lifeblood was experiencing high numbers of cancelations and needed a revamp on their booking experience.

Process: Performed research and analysis to find the real causes why donors were canceling appointments and found the best improvements on their digital experience.

Solution: The goal of these improvements was to reduce the cognitive load of the user, increase their engagement and fast-forward them into a donation chair as soon as possible.

Duration: 2 weeks

Team: Millie, Enza, Hang and Chris

Client: Australia Red Cross

Methods: Market research, Comparative Analysis, User Interview, Affinity Mapping, Personas, Journey Map, Crazy 8s, Design Studio, MVP, Sketching, Wireframing and Prototyping

Tools: Trello, Google Forms, Otter.ia, Miro and Figma

1) Breafing: Australian Red Cross Lifeblood was facing high rates of cancelled appointments, thousands of donors were cancelling their donations every week and 43% of first time donors were not coming back.

'How might we ensure that convenience is at the forefront in the scheduling process?'

2) Challenges: Analyse user behaviour and motivation; Align user needs, business goals and time constraints. Understand client and its market placement.
3) How to tackle those challenges? Primary & secondary research. Having deeper conversations with users. Finding issues and pain points within the current app. Ensuring the team is aligned with the ideas and solutions.
